ⓘ
45 min. Read 6–7 sources and write an argument incorporating at least 3. A score of 6 requires a defensible thesis, skillful use of evidence, and sophisticated reasoning.

ⓘ

ⓘ

Section II Part A — DBQ
25% of score
Thesis / Claim (0–1)
ⓘ
Your thesis must make a historically defensible claim AND establish a line of reasoning. It cannot just restate the prompt. Place it in the intro or conclusion.
1
/ 1
Contextualization (0–1)
ⓘ
Describe a broader historical context that is RELEVANT to the prompt. Must be more than a phrase — explain how the context relates to your argument. This is the most commonly missed point.
1
/ 1
Evidence — Document Use (0–2)
ⓘ
1 pt: accurately use content from at least 3 documents. 2 pts: use at least 6 documents AND explain how each supports your argument. Don't just quote — analyze.
1
/ 2
Evidence — Outside Evidence (0–1)
ⓘ
Use at least one piece of historically accurate evidence NOT found in the documents. Must be relevant and must explain how it supports your argument.
1
/ 1
Sourcing — HAPP Analysis (0–1)
ⓘ
For at least 3 documents, explain the Historical situation, Audience, Purpose, or Point of View AND how it affects the document's meaning or limitations. Must go beyond just naming these.
1
/ 1
Complexity (0–1)
ⓘ
Demonstrate a complex understanding: explain nuance, multiple causes, both similarities AND differences, or how the argument applies across time periods. Must be sustained throughout the essay, not just one sentence.
